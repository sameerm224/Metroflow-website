Context-aware editors

Write with live context from your stack

Metroflow editors are wired to your synced metadata — not a generic language server. When you type {{ ref('stg_orders') }}, the suggestion is validated against your actual dbt manifest and warehouse schema.

SQL, dbt models, Python transforms, and orchestration configs all share the same semantic context. Rename a column upstream and see warnings before you merge — not after a failed nightly run.

Jump from any symbol to its lineage, owners, and freshness without leaving the file. Your IDE finally understands what "production" means in your environment.

  • Autocomplete for refs, sources, and warehouse tables from live crawls
  • Inline lineage preview on hover — upstream models and downstream dashboards
  • Schema drift warnings when a model's columns no longer match the catalog
  • One-click navigation from a failing test to the model and its dependencies
  • Shared context across SQL, YAML, and Python in the same workspace

Living semantic catalog

Lineage that stays fresh automatically

The catalog is not a quarterly documentation sprint. Metroflow crawls Snowflake information schema, dbt manifests, Airflow DAG metadata, and 40+ connectors on a schedule you control — then merges everything into one searchable graph.

Every table, model, column, and job carries ownership, tags, freshness, and governance status. Search "revenue" and get certified metrics, staging models, and raw Stripe tables — with lineage between them, not a Confluence page from 2022.

When a PR merges or a DAG changes, the graph updates. Your team always works against what is actually running — not what someone remembered to document.

  • Automatic discovery from dbt Cloud, Core manifests, and local projects
  • Column-level lineage where connectors expose it — trace KPIs to source fields
  • Ownership and Slack/email contacts surfaced on every asset
  • Freshness badges tied to orchestration runs and warehouse load times
  • Governance tags: PII, certified, deprecated — filterable across the graph
Infrastructure understanding

DAGs, models, and dependencies unified

Most teams keep orchestration in Airflow, transforms in dbt, and tables in Snowflake — with no single view of how a failure in one layer ripples to board-level dashboards. Metroflow stitches those layers into one traversable graph.

Click any node and see upstream sources, downstream consumers, and the orchestration jobs that materialize it. Impact analysis that used to take an afternoon of Slack threads becomes a 30-second trace.

Connector health sits alongside semantic context — so you know whether a stale KPI is a pipeline delay or a definition change, not both at once.

  • Cross-layer traces: Airflow task → dbt model → warehouse table → Looker explore
  • Blast-radius preview before renaming columns or deprecating models
  • Filter by team, domain, or environment — prod vs. staging in one click
  • Sync status per connector with last-crawl timestamp and error details
  • Export lineage subgraphs for compliance reviews and architecture docs
Self-host & local development

Your infrastructure. Your rules.

Metroflow ships Apache 2.0 with Docker Compose for local dev and production-style VPC deployment. Metadata crawls are read-only — credentials and query results never leave your network.

Run the full workspace on a laptop for evaluation, then promote the same images to your Kubernetes cluster or EC2 instance. RBAC, audit logs, and SSO hooks are built in — not gated behind an enterprise tier.

Open source core, forever. No surprise license changes when your team scales from five engineers to fifty.

  • docker compose up — workspace live in under 5 minutes
  • Metadata-only crawls: no row-level data replication to Metroflow servers
  • Full audit trail of queries, agent actions, and definition changes
  • Role-based access aligned to your IdP — engineer, analyst, executive views
  • Air-gapped and on-prem friendly for regulated industries
